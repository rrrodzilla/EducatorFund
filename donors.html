Frequently Asked Questions

What is EducatorFund?

EducatorFund lets anybody gift one-time or monthly payments to educators. Educators use the funds to buy supplies and educational experiences for our children. Ninety-four percent of US educators already do this with their own money. EducatorFund is an artificial intelligence powered SMS chat bot. Our chatbot works over text messages (SMS). You text our bot and our bot texts you back. Sometimes to gather information and other times to process your gift payments. Our software uses a low-fidelity interface (text messages) but packs some smarts!

How do I pay an educator for a class?

EducatorFund assigns every class a number (i.e. 1234) you will use to identify it. Each teacher also receives a unique username. To contribute to a class you'll need three pieces of information.

  • Our contribution phone number (+1 (855) 822-0333)
  • Your educator's username. For example "mathguy" or "coachjoe".
  • The class number you want to contribute to.

Text your educator's username to our contribution phone number. Send the class number when EducatorFund asks for it. Then follow the instructions to make a one-time payment or subscribe monthly to the class. In either case you get to decide how much you contribute. (At least $5 USD)

If this is the first time you are making a contribution then you'll want to have your payment details nearby. The EducatorFund chat bot will call you to get your credit or debit card details. Our payment provider stores your payment details for future use. If you use EducatorFund again, we'll ask you if you want to use the same card again. EducatorFund never stores PCI (Payment Card Industry) data on our own servers. Our payment processor is fully PCI compliant.

What payment methods do you accept?

Users in the United States can accept Visa, Mastercard, American Express, Discover, JCB, Diners Club and China UnionPay credit and debit cards from people anywhere in the world.

What kinds of things can educators use funds for?

Anything. EducatorFund aims to replace the money educators currently pay out-of-pocket. EducatorFund is not a non-profit donation platform. EducatorFund reports gifts provided to educators as 1099 income. EducatorFund does not attempt to restrict educator purchases to curated vendors. We trust educators to use the funds provided by parents, family and others for their students. They are already doing it with their own money today.

How much can I give?

Contributors can give as much as $999,999 USD and as little as $5 USD as either a one-time gift or as an on-going subscription. Contact us for any amounts greater than that range.

Is EducatorFund a non-profit?

No. EducatorFund is created, owned and operated by Neyborly Company, a private technology firm in Seattle, Washington. Neyborly Company relies on revenue from platform and credit card fees to help us pay our technology partners, provide quality customer support, process transfers, and pay for other costs associated with providing our service. Fees are deducted from educator revenues at the time of transfer.

What commands does the EducatorFund bot understand?

EducatorFund will let you know what you can reply with by highlighting commands in ALL CAPS. For example, if EducatorFund replies with: "You can support a NEW class or MANAGE your subscriptions." Then you can respond with either NEW or MANAGE depending on what you want to do. Other times EducatorFund will tell you if it is expecting a number. Occasionally EducatorFund will ask you a yes or no question, and you can reply with either a yes or no. Your replies to EducatorFund do not have to be in all caps.

None. Since EducatorFund works over text messaging, there are no websites to visit (unless you come here for information). No URLs to bookmark. Nothing to install. You're welcome. ;-)

When can I cancel my subscriptions?

Subscriptions to classes can be cancelled at any time by texting HELLO to EducatorFund and replying with option 2. EducatorFund will gather some information from you to cancel your subscription immediately.

Who is an educator?

Educators can be anyone we send our children to for education. Teachers in public or private schools, pre-school through college are obvious candidates. But coaches for youth sports programs can use EducatorFund as well. Little leagues, soccer, football and swimming coaches need easy ways to receive funds. Parent-teacher associations can receive monthly contributions by setting up subscriptions. This is far more powerful than a bake sale. Community youth centers can do the same.

What counts as a class?

A class can be anything that makes sense for an educator to collect funds for. Educators can create as many classes to fund as they desire. One educator might have a class for a specific classroom (Introduction to Art - 5th Period). Or she might create a single class for her entire student population (Art - 2018/2019 school year). One educator might create a class for her team (Little Sharks Community Soccer Team). Another might be a treasurer for a parent teacher association to create a class for the entire organization (Our Middle School PTA Fund).

Why text messaging? Why isn't there an app for this?

We believe in low fidelity user interfaces backed by super-smart software. This allows the most amount of people to enjoy our platform. Did you know that only 77% of the US population has a smart phone of any kind? Coincidentally, only 77% of the US population has broadband internet access. Much of this population resides in 35 of our most affluent cities. 95% of the US population has a mobile phone that sends and receives text messages. This means our platform is accessible to over 70 million people who wouldn't have been able to use it if it were an app. No hardware to install. No software to download. Using EducatorFund couldn't be easier.
