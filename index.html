Frequently Asked Questions

What is EducatorFund?

EducatorFund lets anybody gift one-time or monthly payments to educators. Educators use the funds to buy supplies and educational experiences for our children. Ninety-four percent of US educators already do this with their own money. EducatorFund is an artificial intelligence powered SMS chat bot. Our chatbot works over text messages (SMS). You text our bot and our bot texts you back. Sometimes to gather information and other times to process your gift payments. Our software uses a low-fidelity interface (text messages) but packs some smarts!

What commands does the EducatorFund bot understand?

EducatorFund will let you know what you can reply with by highlighting commands in ALL CAPS. For example, if EducatorFund replies with: "You can add a NEW class or EDIT an existing one." Then you can respond with either NEW or EDIT depending on what you want to do. Other times EducatorFund will tell you if it is expecting a number. Occasionally EducatorFund will ask you a yes or no question, and you can reply with either a yes or no. Your replies to EducatorFund do not have to be in all caps.

How do I sign up?

Text SIGN UP to +1 (855) 217-1927 and follow the instructions the EducatorFund chat bot will send you. Signing up is fast, free and easy! We also recommend saving the EducatorFund phone number in your phone's address book for easy reference.

How do I get funds for my classes?

EducatorFund assigns every class a unique number (i.e. 1234) you will use to identify it. Each educator also chooses a unique username. To contribute to a class donors need three pieces of information.

  • Our contribution phone number (+1 (855) 822-0333)
  • Your educator username. For example "mathguy" or "coachjoe".
  • The class number they want to contribute to.

Contributors text your educator username to our contribution phone number. They send the class number when EducatorFund asks for it. Then they follow the instructions to make a one-time payment or subscribe monthly to your class. In either case they get to decide how much they contribute. (At least $5 USD)

If this is the first time they are making a contribution then our EducatorFund chat bot will call them to get their credit or debit card details. Our payment provider stores their payment details for future use. If they use EducatorFund again, we'll ask if they want to use the same card again. EducatorFund never stores PCI (Payment Card Industry) data on our own servers. Our payment processor is fully PCI compliant.

How much does it cost?

Signing up and using EducatorFund is free for all users. EducatorFund relies on revenue from platform and credit card fees to help us pay our technology partners, provide quality customer support, process transfers, and pay for other costs associated with providing our service. EducatorFund deducts credit card processing fees from each transaction: 2.9% + .30 cents USD. EducatorFund's 12% platform fee is also deducted from each transaction. An additional $2 per month is deducted for active accounts. EducatorFund considers an account active in any month that it receives funds from our platform. If no funds are transferred, then no $2 fee applies. An example: If you recieve a $50 USD contribution and it is your first contribution of January. You will receive $50-1.46 (2.9%) - .3 - 6 (12%) - $2 = $40.25. If you recieve another $50 USD contribution in the same month. You will receive $50-1.46 (2.9%) - .3 - 6 (10%) = $42.25 for a total of $82.50 for January.

How do I get access to the money contributed to my classes?

When you sign up with EducatorFund, you also sign up with Stripe, our payment provider. You'll provide Stripe with a bank account to deposit funds into. When contributors pay your classes, Stripe deposits the money to your account. Stripe makes deposits every day, including weekends, except for bank holidays. Funds deposit on a two-day rolling basis. This means that charges made to customers on Monday get deposited on Wednesday and so on.

What makes EducatorFund different from a crowdfunding platform?

Crowdfunding platforms let donors pledge a certain amount towards funding a specific project. If enough people pledge money to the project then the platform releases all the funds to the project. Educators may receive no money if enough people don't pledge funds.

EducatorFund treats every contribution as a gift or donation to the educator. The IRS considers these contributions additional income so educators recieve a 1099 from EducatorFund at the end of the tax year. Whether an educator receives $5 or $5000 the educator will receive their funds right away. If an art teacher doesn't get enough funds for supplies, he is likely to buy the supplies out-of-pocket. Even a few small contributions helps ease an educator's out-of-pocket expenses.

Crowdfunding platforms also have high minimum funding requirements. Some need projects valued at $300 or more to use the platform. EducatorFund requires a $5 minimum for one-time gifts or ongoing subscriptions from donors.

Other platforms impose spending restrictions on educators. They can only buy certain products from vetted sponsor companies. EducatorFund poses no restrictions on how educators use thier funds. We trust our educators to continue making smart and relevant purchases to enhance our children's educations. As mentioned, almost all educators in the US already do so out-of-pocket.

Who is an educator?

Educators can be anyone we send our children to for education. Teachers in public or private schools, pre-school through college are obvious candidates. But coaches for youth sports programs can use EducatorFund as well. Little leagues, soccer, football and swimming coaches need easy ways to receive funds. Parent-teacher associations can receive monthly contributions by setting up subscriptions. This is far more powerful than a bake sale. Community youth centers can do the same. In short, if you teach children anything at all and incur out-of-pocket costs you'd like assistance with, then EducatorFund is for you.

What counts as a class?

A class can be anything that makes sense for an educator to collect funds for. Educators can create as many classes to fund as they desire. One educator might have a separate class for each classroom (Introduction to Art - 5th Period) and so on. Or she might create a single class for her entire student population (Art - 2018/2019 school year). One educator might create a class for her team (Little Sharks Community Soccer Team). Another might be a treasurer for a parent teacher association to create a class for the entire organization (Our Middle School PTA Fund).

We believe in low fidelity user interfaces backed by super-smart software. This allows the most amount of people to enjoy our platform. This means our platform is accessible to over 70 million people who wouldn't have been able to use it if it were an app. No hardware to install. No software to download. Using EducatorFund couldn't be easier.
